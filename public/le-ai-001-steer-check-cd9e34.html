The principle that shapes this matrix
AI takes the donkey work, not the judgement. Nothing here removes or dilutes an engineer's professional responsibility. The aim is to free the routine effort so chartered engineers focus their judgement where it matters. Opportunities that touch engineering decisions sit in Phase 2, where that trust is built first.

Each bubble is a tool or task opportunity, plotted by business impact and effort to implement. Larger bubbles carry more released capacity. Quick Wins (bottom-right) are judgement-light: AI does the gathering and drafting, the engineer keeps the call. Higher-Value items (top-right) are worth doing but touch engineering judgement, so they follow once trust and governance are in place. This is a DRAFT for direction; positions firm up after tool research.

Tier 1Foundations, the enabling layerMakes every tool win stick firm-wide. An operating system, not a single task.
RefOpportunityWhat it is and why it matters
E-01Training & AI-literacy levellingThe team spans a wide range, from staff building their own automation to some still taking minutes on paper. Bring everyone to a shared baseline. Your AI policy already commits to this training; this delivers it.
E-02Operationalise your AI policyYou have a sound Revision 1 policy. The gap is turning its intent into working practice: the audit trail it asks for, the report-QA checklist it assumes, a clear tool-approval route. Strengthen what exists, do not start over.
E-03SOPs & standardisationCapture what the internal team has already built, plus the recommended ways of working, as repeatable standard procedures, using a proven linked-skills method so good practice is not locked in one person's head.
E-04Stepped test-and-verify governanceA clear pilot-then-rollout gate: prove each tool in one team, settle the checks, then scale. Operationalises your own risk tiers (low, medium, high, prohibited) so adoption is safe and consistent.

Foundations need an owner with a mandate. Your policy names an AI Sponsor at director level, the right place for accountability. What sits underneath, and is not yet resourced, is the day-to-day implementation capacity to deliver E-01 to E-04. I will return to how that capacity is best provided.
